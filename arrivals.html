Arrivals –Departures

The arrival of the HMT Empire Windrush at Tilbury Docks in Essex in 1948 has acquired iconic status, lending its name to what is widely known as the ‘Windrush generation’, a term that refers to the postwar history of West Indian immigration into England. This collection includes the report on the arrival of this ship in 1948, but it also includes reports on the arrival and departure of other ships to and from England in the 1940s-1950s. In other words, this collection covers news stories about migration as a two-way process, one of emigration from and immigration to the UK. Find out how reporting on emigration from the UK differs from news stories on immigration in its tone, language and emphasis.
