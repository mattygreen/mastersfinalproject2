When does the word “anti-racist” seem to appear for the first time on Radio 4? And in relation to what?
This collection covers news stories about Black Power and figures such as Robert Williams, Enos Beach, Stokely Carmichael, Malcolm X, Michael Malik (or, Michael de Frietas or Michael X) and Bobby Seale.
Our contemporary understanding of Black Power is that of resisting White power and achieving self-determination for Black people. Find out here what Radio 4’s reporting on Black Power figures in the 1960s and 1970s focused on, as well as the tone and language used. Other major news stories in this collection are the fire inquest into the deaths of 13 young Black people in a house fire in New Cross in 1981, the first year anniversary of this tragedy and the efforts of the New Cross Massacre Action Committee to find justice. You can also hear from Robert Ruddock who lost two members of his family in the fire. He gives his account of the events and his thoughts on the BBC’s reporting style and wider media coverage of the fire.
